He is an economist and social entrepreneur who is best known for his work in the field of microfinance, microcredit, and the creation of the Grameen Bank. He is also known as the father of microcredit which is followed by more than 100 countries all over the world including the United States. His work has been recognized globally and his Grameen Social Business Model has been adopted by universities, entrepreneurs, and corporations across the world. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in 2006, along with Grameen Bank, for their efforts to create economic and social development.
